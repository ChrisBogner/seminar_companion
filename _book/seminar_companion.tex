\hypertarget{curriculum-and-grading}{%
\section{Curriculum and grading}\label{curriculum-and-grading}}

This course is part of either \emph{Fachinhaltliche Vertiefung I} or \emph{Fachinhaltliche Vertiefung II}. Its main rational is to give you the opportunity to delve into a exciting topic of your choice. You will

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  present this topic to your peers (30\% of your mark)
\item
  write a critical literature review on it (70\% of your mark)
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{intended-learning-outcomes}{%
\section{Intended learning outcomes}\label{intended-learning-outcomes}}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Apply structural reading (SQ4R) to scientific literature.
\item
  Present professionally and discuss a topic in the scope of the seminar.
\item
  Write a critical literature review on a topic in the scope of the seminar.
\item
  Apply methods of scientific writing to your seminar paper

  \begin{itemize}
  \tightlist
  \item
    systematic literature search using data bases
  \item
    selection of relevant literature
  \item
    summary of research results avoiding plagiarism
  \item
    critical evaluation
  \end{itemize}
\item
  Give peer feedback to your peers' work professionally in written form.
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{presenting-student}{%
\section{Presenting student}\label{presenting-student}}

We will establish more detailed criteria of a good scientific presentation during the seminar. Thus, the following requirements are general and a sctrict minimum:

\textbf{Content}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Title slide (title, your name, title of the seminar)
\item
  Motivation slide: Why is this topic relevant and why does it need a critical review
\item
  Body of your presentation: Here, you give information on your topic
\item
  Ciritical evaluation or discussion
\item
  References: you prepare, but do not show this slide
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Time}
You have 15 minutes to present your topic. Afterwords, we will have a group discussion.

\textbf{Feedback}
You will receive feedback from your peers in form of one-minute papers.
