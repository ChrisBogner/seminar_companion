\hypertarget{presentation}{%
\chapter{Presentation}\label{presentation}}

\hypertarget{presenting-student}{%
\section{Presenting student}\label{presenting-student}}

We will establish more detailed criteria of a good scientific presentation during the seminar. Thus, the following requirements are general and a strict minimum:

\textbf{Content}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Title slide (title, your name, title of the seminar)
\item
  Motivation slide: Why is this topic relevant and why does it need a critical review
\item
  Body of your presentation: Here, you give information on your topic
\item
  Critical evaluation or discussion
\item
  Take-home messages: Summarize the most important aspects
\item
  References: you prepare, but do not show this slide
\end{itemize}

\begin{rmdalert}
Cite the literature and all figures/tables/data you use correctly!
\end{rmdalert}

\textbf{Time}

You have \textbf{15 minutes} to present your topic. Afterwords, we will have a group discussion.

\textbf{Feedback}

You will receive feedback from your peers in form of one-minute papers through campuswire. Therefore, in \texttt{class\ feed}, post the title of your presentation. Your peers will respond to this post with their feedback.

\hypertarget{peers}{%
\section{Peers}\label{peers}}

Listen carefully and critically to the presenter and provide \emph{anonymous} feedback through campuswire. Structure your feedback as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  The main points of the presentation: Summarize what you understood in 2--3 sentences. The presenter should see whether his/her take-home messages were received.
\item
  State which concrete issues you observed (see feedback rules below)
\item
  Suggest concrete ways to improve and further aspects to include in the review paper.
\end{enumerate}

\hypertarget{feedback-rules}{%
\section{Feedback rules}\label{feedback-rules}}

\hypertarget{how-to-give-feedback}{%
\subsection{How to give feedback}\label{how-to-give-feedback}}

A peer feedback should always be provided as a \emph{personal opinion} and communicated accordingly. A peer feedback, both written and oral is

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  respectful
\item
  me-centered
\item
  addresses a concrete observation
\item
  tells what it did to the feedback provider
\item
  provides a doable suggestion
\end{itemize}

\begin{rmdexample}
On slide 4, you explained that carbon sequestration in the soil depended
on temperature. I am not familiar with this and did not understand the
relationship to climate change. Could you please explain this in your
paper in more detail. For your paper, you could also include the aspect
of increasing CO\textsubscript{2} in the atmosphere and ocean
acidification.
\end{rmdexample}

\hypertarget{how-to-receive-feedback}{%
\subsection{How to receive feedback}\label{how-to-receive-feedback}}

Remember that the feedback is a \emph{personal opinion}. Therefore,

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  don't take it personally
\item
  don't apologize
\item
  take it and say thank you
\item
  you decide whether you want to implement this feedback
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{general-structure-and-length-of-your-review}{%
\section{General structure and length of your review}\label{general-structure-and-length-of-your-review}}

Your review must be structured as follows:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Title page: contains your name, the title of the seminar, the name of your lecturer and the submission date
\item
  Abstract
\item
  Key words
\item
  Table of content
\item
  Introduction
\item
  Method
\item
  Main part
\item
  Discussion
\item
  Conclusions
\item
  References
\end{itemize}

Your review should be between 3000 and 6000 words in the main part (i.e.~without abstract, table of contents, references etc.).

\hypertarget{software}{%
\section{Software}\label{software}}

You can select any free or commercial word processing software, e.g. \href{https://www.libreoffice.org/}{Libre Office}, \href{http://www.openoffice.org/de/}{Open Office}, \href{https://www.latex-project.org/}{LaTeX} or \href{https://rrzk.uni-koeln.de/software-multimedia/software/rahmenvertraege-und-campuslizenzen/microsoft/office-fuer-studierende}{Word}. However, be sure to correctly \textbf{connect your word processor to your bibliography manager}. Do not copy and paste references manually because this is very error prone and you risk to forget citations.

You are also free to select any reference management software. The free \href{https://www.zotero.org/}{Zotero} is recommended, but the UoC also provides licences for \href{https://rrzk.uni-koeln.de/software-multimedia/software/rahmenvertraege-und-campuslizenzen/citavi}{Citavi} and \href{https://rrzk.uni-koeln.de/software-multimedia/software/rahmenvertraege-und-campuslizenzen/endnote}{Endnote} to manage your bibliography.

In case you decide to use Zotero, there is a helpful \href{https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hm0TboOcAuM\&list=PLScSkCQ_d7EOSU6n682bwjTC6GjY_ihmM}{video tutorial} explaining how to use it and how to connect it to your word processing software.

We will use a citation style close to the one used by the journal Geomorphology. You can download the style for Zotero \href{https://www.zotero.org/styles?q=elsevier\&dependent=0}{here} and for Endnote \href{https://endnote.com/style_download/geomorphology/}{here}. If you want to use Citavi, you have to \href{https://www1.citavi.com/sub/manual5/en/cse_finding_citation_styles.html}{find} an appropriate style yourself.

\hypertarget{plagiarism}{%
\section{Plagiarism}\label{plagiarism}}

You will have a blended learning unit on plagiarism and how to avoid it. We will exercise paraphrasing during the seminar.

I will check your paper draft for plagiarism with \href{https://www.turnitin.com/}{turnitin} and give you detailed feedback. The final paper will also be checked for plagiarism. Plagiarism will result in a worse grade and substantial plagiarism in failing the seminar.
