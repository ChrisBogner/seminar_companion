2  Systematic literature review

2.1 PRISMA appraoch

In this seminar, we will follow the PRISMA approach to write the systematic literature review.

2.2 Literature search

We will use the following literature databases mandatorily:

  1. Web of Science
  2. Scopus

You can complement your literature search by:

  • Google Scholar
  • Research Rabit
  • Eilicit
  • Incietful
  • …

For Web of Science, either create an account and link it to UoC or activate your VPN client when accessing from outside the university.

Similarly, to access Scopus, you will need your VPN client.

2.3 General structure and length of your review

Your review must be structured as follows:

  • Title page contains: your name, your matriculation number, the title of the seminar, the name of your lecturer and the submission date
  • Abstract: write a structured abstract. It consists of background information, knowledge gap, methods used, results, conclusions. Every point is 2–3 sentences long. In other words, you rewrite your paper in miniature format.
  • Keywords: 4–6
  • Table of content
  • Introduction: provide the background for your topic. Why is it relevant? Why is a review necessary? What is the research question or the goal of your review?
  • Method: summarize your literature search here. When did you the search? In which databases? Which keywords did you use? How did you exclude irrelevant papers?
  • Main part: you can name it differently, depending on your topic. In this part, you present your results. In most cases, you will subdivide this section in several subsections
  • Discussion: Discuss critically your topic and the results you have summarized in the Main part. The focus is not on rating the papers, but on critical assessment of their content. Answer your research questions!
  • Conclusions: Summary and take-home messages.
  • References

If you use many abbreviations, put a list of abbreviations after the keywords.

Your review should be between 4000 and 6000 words (excluding abstract, keywords, table of contents, list of abbreviations and references).

2.4 Software

You can select any free or commercial word-processing software, e.g. LibreOffice, Open Office, LaTeX or MS Word. However, be sure to correctly connect your word processor to your bibliography manager. Do not copy and paste references manually because this is very error-prone, and you risk forgetting citations.

You are also free to select any reference management software. The free Zotero is recommended, but the UoC also provides licences for Citavi and Endnote to manage your bibliography.

In case you decide to use Zotero, there is a helpful video tutorial explaining how to use it and how to connect it to your word-processing software.

We will use the citation style Elsevier - Vancouver (author-date; sorted alphabetical) In Zotero and Geomorphology in Endnote. You can download the style for Zotero here and for Endnote here. If you want to use Citavi, you have to find an appropriate style yourself.
